## Hey, fellow Android gamer!

If your phone’s storage is already gasping under the weight of massive mainstream titles, it might be time to look elsewhere. The indie scene on Android has been quietly flourishing, and this April 2026 brought us an especially exciting wave of creative, lightweight games that won’t break the bank—or your device’s memory.

Below, I’ve handpicked seven indie titles that stood out this month. Each one brings something genuinely fresh to the table, whether you’re into tactical battles, word puzzles, or deep RPG storytelling. Let’s jump right in.

---

## Sea of Stars

:::figure
[![Sea of Stars](https://play-lh.googleusercontent.com/cp6tR3siCcRhJZqZm7ZLpHkxMkr2glN7sNtHZ7Iq2VmGTfrRhVeGFK154JqD8HJx34sYStKH1PZY0gqjGWl2rXc=w2560-h1440-rw "View on Playstore")](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.playdigious.seaofstars&pcampaignid=web_share)
:::

If you grew up with timeless JRPGs like *Chrono Trigger*, *Sea of Stars* will hit you right in the nostalgia. Developed by Sabotage Studio and already celebrated on PC and consoles, it officially arrived on Android on April 7, 2026.

What makes it special is how it modernizes the classic formula. Combat is turn-based but demands precise timing for blocks and combos, keeping every encounter engaging. Exploration feels fluid, and the story weaves together a rich tapestry of friendship and cosmic stakes. The mobile port doesn’t disappoint either—the touch controls are intuitive, and it fully supports external controllers for a console-like experience. At around $10.99 USD, this is a premium adventure well worth the investment.

Promising a touch of Sabotage in every system, Sea of ​​Stars aims to modernize the classic RPG formula in terms of turn-based combat, storytelling, exploration, and interaction with the environment, while still offering a strong sense of nostalgia and classic simple fun.

## Drova - Forsaken Kin

:::figure
[![Drova - Forsaken Kin](https://play-lh.googleusercontent.com/sZIAKBIK9f-RUOP6g2W7zWZYsmZCMK_kGE7P-tuGvzkBAlxBcjC3bf3d7MFWIz4xVQfinxplbYfVp8FOfsEWc7Y=w2560-h1440-rw "View on Playstore")](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.Just2D.DrovaMobile&pcampaignid=web_share)
:::

On the opposite end of the RPG spectrum, *Drova - Forsaken Kin* trades bright fantasy for a grim world steeped in Celtic mythology. Think of it as a pocket-sized *Gothic*—dark, unforgiving, and beautifully rendered in pixel art.

Your choices carry real weight here, actively shaping the narrative and its multiple endings. The sense of danger is ever-present; you’re not a chosen hero but a survivor clawing your way through a hostile land. Released on April 23, 2026, *Drova* is priced at €9.99 (roughly $10.99 USD) and supports controllers from day one. Perfect for those who prefer their RPGs bleak and atmospheric.

"Drova - Forsaken Kin" is a pixel art style Action-RPG inspired by the classics of the dark genre and the mystical fascination of Celtic mythology. ## Neverness to Everness

:::figure
[![Neverness to Everness](https://cdn.c0desk1.my.id/img/AgACAgUAAxkBAANHafMiIqcbd02NQkyAOFuiHNHtLzQAAtQQaxsYJZlXESO-pBm-4D0BAAMCAAN5AAM7BA "View on Playstore")](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.hottagames.nte&pcampaignid=web_share)
:::

Dubbed by many as “Anime GTA,” *Neverness to Everness* (NTE) from Hotta Studio flings you into Hethereau, a vibrant futuristic city teeming with anomalies. You play as an “Anomaly Hunter,” but how you spend your time is largely up to you.

The freedom here is staggering. You can tear through neon-lit streets in customizable cars, purchase and decorate properties, run businesses, or simply explore every hidden corner of the sprawling map. One standout feature is full cross-progression—start on your phone, then seamlessly continue on PC or PS5. The game launched on April 29, 2026, and early adopters even received a free character as a launch gift. For open-world enthusiasts looking for a massive sandbox on mobile, NTE sets a new bar.

:::banner
:title[Neverness to Everness]
![Neverness to Everness](https://play-lh.googleusercontent.com/TGl3DdDbi4hty6ooOhEvv_2s5tkpGLbL6derP8y5hK-_Bw--Ve375b08ba9HHQ9ueRpoQ9oye1T_T24BJriLUmw=s48-rw)
NTE is a supernatural open-world RPG developed by Hotta Studio. The story begins in the town of Hethereau, where you play as the city's first "unlicensed" Anomaly Hunter. You'll join the crew at Eibon, a dilapidated antique shop that survives by accepting Anomaly commissions from anyone willing to pay. Along with a diverse cast of fantastical companions, you'll uncover urban mysteries, embark on adventures filled with laughter and heartbreak, and write your own story on neon-lit streets.
[Download on Play Store](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.hottagames.nte&pcampaignid=web_share)

## Gambonanza

:::figure
[![Gambonanza](https://cdn.c0desk1.my.id/img/AgACAgUAAxkBAANJafMiReFamhfZ9jXx8dQNLSAiZ0wAAtUQaxsYJZlXxQABFGGj18gDAQADAgADeQADOwQ "View on Playstore")](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.strayfawnstudio.gambonanza&pcampaignid=web_share)
:::

At first glance, *Gambonanza* might look like a quirky board game, but underneath its cheerful exterior lies a deeply strategic chess-based roguelike. Crafted by a solo developer, this title is as niche as it is brilliant.

You’ll navigate a series of procedurally generated boards, moving chess pieces with their traditional rules to overcome enemies and obstacles. The tactical depth ramps up quickly, demanding careful planning and creative use of each piece’s strengths. It’s a genuine mental workout disguised as a lighthearted romp. Released at the very end of April (officially May 1, 2026), *Gambonanza* is a breath of fresh air amidst a sea of generic mobile titles, especially if you enjoy strategy that makes you pause and think.

:::banner
:title[Gambonanza]
![Gambonanza](https://play-lh.googleusercontent.com/aPeUytf5y1c0YCwU-hh1G3vULaPCLnoi5BXrQZWNJ3BH0FqVLXU6AX5i7c8mFkTD5eeOwjzhDHOoj9T_T3UhcrE=s48-rw)
Gambonanza is a turn-based roguelike chess game played on a small board with high stakes. Master complex tactics, invest your winnings, and discover rule-breaking gambits and wild tile and piece combinations. Face challenging bosses and discover countless ways to break traditional chess rules!

## ~The Rhymatory~

~Need something to wind down with after a hectic day? *The Rhymatory* is a delightful word puzzle game set in a cozy steampunk world. You’re joined by a friendly ruby, an owl, and a crow who happily brew you tea while you solve over 300 rhyming puzzles.~

~The premise is simple: you’re given clues and must find the rhyming word pair that fits. But the cleverness of the puzzles and the warm, gentle atmosphere make it surprisingly hard to put down. There’s no timer, no penalty, just pure relaxed solving. It came out on April 30, 2026, and best of all, it’s free to play—a charming, low-commitment addition to any phone.~


## Hearthside: A Teeny Tiny Game

:::figure
[![Hearthside: A Teeny Tiny Game](https://cdn.c0desk1.my.id/img/AgACAgUAAxkBAANNafMijZ2kGqbm9rA-BkXiYFSplZYAAtcQaxsYJZlXMcjn_ien9xIBAAMCAAN5AAM7BA "View on Playstore")](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.shortcircuitstudio.hearthside&hl=id)
:::

Fans of cozy experiences like *Animal Crossing* or *Stardew Valley* will instantly fall for *Hearthside*. It’s a tile-placement puzzle and city-builder hybrid where you construct an adorable little town, tile by tile.

What makes it addictive is the gentle strategy involved. You need to carefully plan where each building, tree, or path goes to help your settlement flourish. The best part? If your layout doesn’t work out, there’s zero punishment—you simply reset and try a new approach. It’s a soothing, low-pressure game perfect for filling short breaks or lazy afternoons at home. A tiny game, indeed, but one with a big heart.

From the creators of Teeny Tiny Town comes a city-building game with enhanced merging, where careful placement and clever combinations turn humble beginnings into a thriving settlement.

## Annulus

:::figure
![Annulus](https://play-lh.googleusercontent.com/7MC3kAHckqlDg7xzc_BPm1dDYErcr7ZTkEEIPR3KMYm9LRfLD_ddmTAa3mFtd-V_mSfkbaaLYKgkIs1IBzfXKA=w2560-h1440-rw)
:::

If you lean more toward dark fantasy and tactical depth, *Annulus* deserves a spot on your home screen. It’s a strategy RPG where you lead a band of mercenaries through the grim, unforgiving continent of Novisess.

What immediately sets *Annulus* apart on mobile is its visual ambition. Powered by Unreal Engine, the game boasts cinematic cutscenes and richly detailed battlefields that feel closer to a PC strategy title than a typical phone game. Gameplay revolves around positioning, resource management, and unlocking the lore hidden in the shadows. Launched on April 8, 2026, it’s a must-play for strategy fans who appreciate atmosphere and weighty decision-making.
